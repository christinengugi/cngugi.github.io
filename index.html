I am a Sophomore at the University of Pennsylvania from Nairobi, Kenya. I am passionate about technology and its abilities to bring about change in Africa. Technology is very dear to me because I see it as what opened up the path for me to move beyond the constraints of my humble beginnings. Projects

TwitterBot

This is a Java scripted bot that is able to generate tweets that seem realistic. This project deals with reading tweets rom CSV files, train the technology to behave in this way, then using the created AI to generate the tweets.


Pennstagram

This is a photo manipulation program, borrowed from the idea of the App "Instagram" (hence the name). I use features such as image rotation, making of borders, contrasting the image and blurring to make the program fit to the needs of various preferred photo manipulation features.


Penn Pals

This is a program that runs a ditributed system that requires a large number of computers to connect. My program allows this system to moniture various groups that people are able to join, and keep track of the memnbers and the owners of the various channels in this program. There are several features in it, such as Changing the Nickname of a user, joining a cfhannel, leaving a chgannel and inviting a user to a channel. This program utilizes a server model to communicate with the various Channels and maintain the data of the peiople in the channels.


Caesar Cipher

Caesar Cipher models a very old enryption technique used in passing coded information. This program reads text files that are encrypted and it decrypts them and vice versa.


Rivalry

This program models a game between the University of Pennsylvania and Princeton University. The movement of the flags move arbitrarily however the University of Pennsylvania has a greater chance of winning in all rounds. The flags also stop when one gets to the finish line and the winner is displayed on the screen.
